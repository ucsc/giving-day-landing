Giving Day Projects List

  • Campuswide Projects
  • Restoring the Quarry Ampitheater

    The Quarry Amphitheater dramatically connects the UC Santa Cruz campus to its history, natural landscape, and rich culture. This iconic and beloved space, now closed, requires a major renovation. On Giving Day there will be a one-to-one incentive match!

  • Area of Greatest Need

    Gifts to the UC Santa Cruz Fund allow the Chancellor’s Office the flexibility to support priorities that advance the values that have made us independent, nimble, and visionary.

  • Division Projects
  • Baskin School of Engineering: Girls in Engineering

    Girls in Engineering is a 5-day, non-residential summer program where we invite local 7th and 8th grade girls to the UC Santa Cruz campus. On Giving Day, The Peggy and Jack Baskin Foundation will offer a one-to-one incentive match, up to $25,000!

  • Physical and Biological Sciences: The Academic Excellence Program

    ACE is an academic support program that is dedicated to increasing the diversity of UCSC students earning a bachelor’s degree in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

  • Humanities: The Gail Project

    The Gail Project is a collaborative, international public history project that explores the founding years of the American military occupation of Okinawa. The project emphasizes hands-on research and creation of content by undergraduate students and serves as an innovative platform for new educational methods that encourage the use of multimedia, social media, archival research and travel.

  • Arts: Dean's Fund for Excellence

    The Arts Dean’s Fund for Excellence is entirely donor funded. It supports undergraduate and graduate students presenting research at professional conferences, research and creative projects by faculty, and programs bringing theater and music into regional schools.

  • Social Sciences: Awards and Fellowships

    Funds raised for the Social Sciences Division will provide awards and fellowships for undergraduate and graduate students. including the Gabriel Zimmerman Memorial Scholarship and the Walsh Award.

  • Library: Other Minds Collection

    Other Minds, the nonprofit dedicated to new and experimental music produced in the past 50 years, donated its archives to UC Santa Cruz in 2016. Next task: digitizing the extraordinary and groundbreaking music from in this unique collection of work artists ranging from John Cage to Pauline Oliveros.

  • Graduate Student Internships

    The Graduate Division is fundraising for internships for graduate students. Internships help build real-world skills—from collaboration to project management to innovation within or outside of academia. These opportunities provide crucial financial help and supporting relationship-building and professional development.

  • Colleges Projects
  • Merrill College: Experential Learning

    Gifts support transformative hands-on student experiences by expanding programs for engagement and educational pursuits. Access to mentorships, field study, meaningful volunteer opportunities are the types of opportunities Merrill College is providing students.

  • Crown College: Paid Student Internships

    Crown sponsors a class that helps students explore the job market and a leadership certificate program that offers opportunities for active participation in our community. It is becoming a hub for opportunities aimed at promoting innovation and entrepreneurship through our partnership with the Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurial Development.

  • Porter College: Creative Studies

    Porter College is fundraising to support the Pavel Machotka Chairs in Creative Studies. The chair holders promote collaboration between students and faculty on research and creative projects that contribute to the college's undergraduate mission.

  • Porter College: Creative Studies

    Porter College is fundraising to support the Pavel Machotka Chairs in Creative Studies. The chair holders promote collaboration between students and faculty on research and creative projects that contribute to the college's undergraduate mission.

  • Kresge College: Kresge Lab

    Kresge Lab is a new concept at our college — a series of courses that emphasize participatory learning, hands-on experience, and courses of study that exceed the walls of traditional classrooms. Our goal is to fund four new 2-credit courses that will greatly expand the Kresge curriculum.

  • Oakes: Poetry Workshop

    This weekly workshop is part of the Oakes-based Westside Writing Center. Students will develop their poetic voice, learn to create vivid imagery, and gain an understanding of the nuances of language in poetry as they explore themes such as identity, nature, place, and memory.

  • Faculty and Staff Initiatives
  • Treehouse Childhood Cancer Project

    The UC Santa Cruz Treehouse Cancer Project enables sharing of pediatric cancer genomic data. But more than that, it makes it possible to analyze a child's cancer data against both childhood and adult patient cohorts across all types of cancer. The childhood cancer project is part of the UC Santa Cruz Genomics Institute.

  • Leaded Eagles: Determining lead exposure in eagles

    This project will reconstruct the lead exposure history in feathers collected from ~10 eagles to evaluate lead exposure rates in free-flying eagles, similar to our work on California condors. To date no one has evaluated the lead exposure history in eagles, yet we know that eagles are lead poisoned around the country.

  • Undocumented Student Services

    Undocumented Student Services works to ensure the success and graduation of undocumented students at UC Santa Cruz. It also provides support through peer mentoring, career opportunities, community building, legal services, and navigating the system of higher education. Donations will go towards scholarships for students, projects, internships, operational needs, and other costs that support the mission of the program.

  • Student Projects
  • Pre-Dental Society

    The Pre-Dental Society is raising money to purchase oral hygiene supplies -- toothbrushes, toothpaste, floss, mouthwash, and tongue scrapers -- to donate around the Santa Cruz community.

  • Senior Class Legacy Fund

    The Senior Legacy Fund is the only student-initiated, student-supported, and student-distributed scholarship at UC Santa Cruz. Contributions will be used by the Classs of 2016 to award scholarships to students who represent the four tenets of S.L.U.G. life: Spirit, Legacy, Unity, and Giving.

  • Haluan Hip Hop Dance Troupe

    Haluan has been gaining recognition in the hip-hop dance community, and has opportunities to perform at shows and competitions in and outside of Santa Cruz. Donations will go toward costs for the troupe, such as competition fees, costumes and props for performances, transportation costs, guest choreographers, and studio space for practices.

  • Hermanas Unidas

    Hermanas Unidas provides resources and leadership opportunities for Chicana and Latina college students and alumni. It is raising funds for its annual retreat, banquet, and scholarhsip for a high school student who represents the mission of the organization.

  • People of Color Sustainability Collective

    The People of Color Sustainabilty Collective (PoCSC) is a collaborative campus effort between the Ethnic Resource Centers, Colleges Nine and Ten, and the Campus Sustainability Office. It showcases the efforts that people of color have made, are making, and will make to advance sustainability locally, nationally, and globally.

  • Smith Renaissance Society

    The Smith Renaissance Society is raising funds for summer and transitional housing for students who lack traditional family support afforded to most of their peers.

  • IDEASS and Sustainability Studies

    Engineering and Sustainability through Student Service (IDEASS) is a service-learning program designed to empower students as change agents to face challenges of the 21st century. IDEASS draws from multiple disciplines to produce projects that combine cutting-edge technology with responsiveness to social, ecological, and economic issues. Contributions will help fund those projects.

  • KZSC Radio

    KZSC (88.1 FM) is an eclectic, educational, non-commercial public radio station that serves as a training ground for UCSC students interested in broadcasting. Funds raised will support our continued commitment to teaching a new generation of media makers.

  • Sports and Recreation

  • Men's Club Soccer

  • Friends Groups Projects

    The Women's Club is creating a scholarship for a re-entry student to commemorate the club's 50th anniversary.

  • Osher Lifelong Learners

  • Farm and Garden

    The Farm and Garden project is fundraising for student engagement through outreach and intern programs and hosting workshops for the community.

  • Arboretum

    Gifts will support renovation of the Arboretum entry to create an enhanced visitor experience.Through this intensive, hands-on, experiential process, students will be exposed to practical horticulture in the context of landscape design, site preparation, and installation of featured plants from our unique collections.

  • Lick Observatory

    Gifts to the Lick Observatory will be used to support preservating the Lick Historical Collections, installing a self-guided summit tour on Mount Hamilton, launching the Saturday Stars program, hosting the annual Shane Observational Astronomy workshop for UC graduate students, and enhancing the Shane 120-inch telescope.

  • The Dickens Project

    The Dickens Project, based at UC Santa Cruz, consists of faculty and graduate students from major American and international universities commited to the study and the author's work and impact. Funds contributed to the Dickens Project will create scholarships for high school students to attend Dickens Universe, a weeklong summer conference.

  • Retireees Silver Slug Award

    The Silver Slug Award is a scholarship awarded to current and retired UC Santa Cruz staff and their dependents who are in need of financial assistance as they seek a degree at UCSC.
