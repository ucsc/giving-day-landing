The Campaign for UC Santa Cruz

  • Campuswide Projects
  • Restoring the Quarry Amphitheater

    The Quarry Amphitheater dramatically connects the UC Santa Cruz campus to its history, natural landscape, and rich culture. This iconic and beloved space, now closed, requires a major renovation. On Giving Day there will be a one-to-one incentive match, up to $12,000!

  • Area of Greatest Need

    Gifts to this fund allow the Chancellor’s Office the flexibility to support top priorities that continue the values that have made us independent, nimble, and visionary.

  • Division Projects
  • Baskin School of Engineering: Girls in Engineering

    Girls in Engineering is a 5 day non-residential summer program where we invite local 7th and 8th grade girls to the UC Santa Cruz campus. On Giving Day The Peggy and Jack Baskin Foundation will offer a one-to-one incentive match, up to $25,000!

  • Physical and Biological Sciences: The Academic Excellence Program (ACE)

    ACE is an academic support program that is dedicated to increasing the diversity of UCSC students earning a bachelor’s degree in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

  • Humanities: The Gail Project

    The Gail Project is a collaborative, international public history project that explores the founding years of the American military occupation of Okinawa. The project emphasizes hands-on research and creation of content by undergraduate students and serves as an innovative platform for new educational methods that encourage the use of multimedia, social media, archival research and travel.

  • Library: Other Minds Collection

    Other Minds, the nonprofit dedicated to new and experimental music produced in the past 50 years, donated its archives to UC Santa Cruz in 2016. Next task: digitizing the extraordinary and groundbreaking music from in this unique collection of work artists ranging from John Cage to Pauline Oliveros.

  • Colleges Projects
  • Merrill College: Experiential learning

    Enabling transformative hands-on experiences by expanding programs for students to engage with their world and educational pursuits. Access to mentorships, field study, meaningful volunteer opportunities are the types of opportunities Merrill College is providing their students.

  • Crown College: Paid student internships

    Crown sponsors a class that helps students explore the job market, a leadership certificate program that offer opportunities for active participation in our community, and is becoming a hub for opportunities aimed at promoting innovation and entrepreneurship through our partnership with the Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurial Development.

  • Student Projects
  • Pre-Dental Society

    This group is raising money to purchase oral hygiene supplies to donate around the Santa Cruz community.

  • Senior Legacy Fund

    Senior Legacy Fund

  • Golden Bear Sequencing

    Sequencing the genome of Monarch, the last of the California Golden bears.

  • Leaded Eagles: Determining lead exposure histories in eagles

    This project will reconstruct the lead exposure history in feathers collected from ~10 eagles to evaluate the use of feathers for determining lead exposure rates in free-flying eagles, similar to our work on California condors. To date no one has evaluated the lead exposure history in eagles, yet we know that eagles are lead poisoned around the country.

  • Haluan Hip Hop Dance Troupe

    Haluan has been gaining a lot of recognition in the hip-hop dance community, and has been given a lot of opportunities to perform at shows and competitions in and outside of Santa Cruz. The donations will go towards the costs for the team, such as: Competition fees, costumes for performances, props for performances, transportation costs, guest choreographers, and reserving studio space for practices.
