  • Campuswide Projects
  • Restoring the Quarry Amphitheater

    The Quarry Amphitheater dramatically connects the UC Santa Cruz campus to its history, natural landscape, and rich culture. This iconic and beloved space, now closed, requires a major renovation. Reopening the Quarry is a featured initiative of the Campaign for UC Santa Cruz. On Giving Day, there is a one-to-one match fund of $12,000.

  • Campaign Initiatives
  • Treehouse Childhood Cancer Project

    The UC Santa Cruz Treehouse Cancer Project enables sharing of pediatric cancer genomic data. It makes it possible to analyze a child's cancer data against both childhood and adult patient cohorts across all types of cancer in the critical search for life-saving treatment options. The Treehouse project is part of the Genomics Institute initiative in the Campaign for UC Santa Cruz.

  • Elephant seals: Weighing the Weanlings

    The Año Nuevo Natural Reserve is home to nearly 10,000 northern elephant seals who travel thousands of miles each year. They congregate back at Año Nuevo, where up to 2,000 pups are born each winter. To better understand how long they live and navigate in the open ocean we have created a “weanling weighing” team. The team will take body measurements, tag, and weigh 300 weaned pups (weanlings) and monitor their health throughout their lives. This research is part of the Coastal Sustainabily initiative in the Campaign for UC Santa Cruz.

  • IAS: Crochet Coral Reef

    The Institute of the Arts and Sciences (IAS) offers innovative public programing that explores who we are, where we came from, and what lies ahead. Your donation will help fund our upcoming exhibition of the acclaimed international Crochet Coral Reef at the Sesnon Gallery and Seymour Marine Discovery Center, and our related interactive projects. The IAS is a signature initiative in the Campaign for UC Santa Cruz. On Giving Day, there is a one-to-one match fund of $1,500.

    The Gail Project is a collaborative, international public history project that explores the founding years of the American military occupation of Okinawa. The project emphasizes hands-on research and creation of content by undergraduate students and serves as an innovative platform for new educational methods that encourage the use of multimedia, social media, archival research and travel. On Giving Day, there is a one-to-one match fund of $5,000.

You will receive a report on the student or students who benefit from your support.

  • Faculty and Staff Initiatives
  • Gene Splicing Lab

    Undergraduate research is a vital part of our life sciences curriculum at UC Santa Cruz. Your gift will go toward providing 10 to 20 students with the supplies needed to carry out hypothesis driven research. The research concerns previously identified genetic variants associated with human disease that are likely to be detrimental to gene expression.Your support will provide students with the supplies needed to carry out this research project.

  • Lead Exposure in Eagles

    The Leaded Eagle project will reconstruct the lead exposure history in feathers collected from ~10 eagles to evaluate lead exposure rates in free-flying eagles, similar to our work on California condors. To date no one has evaluated the lead exposure history in eagles, yet we know that eagles are lead poisoned around the country.

  • iGEM Competition

    The International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) Competition brings together teams from around the world to create radical novel solutions to problems of our modern society. The UCSC iGEM team is working on a set of projects which will revolutionize sustainable future development in food production and safety, production of industrial goods, and energy, using the nascent field of synthetic biology to integrate California's agricultural heritage with the emerging bioeconomy.

  • The Bird School Project

    Alumni Darrow Feldstein and Kevin Condon began The Bird School Project as an effort to inspire students and teachers to develop a scientific understanding and appreciation for their local birds and habitats. Now, The Bird School Project is seeking funds to help expand their program and support experiential, inquiry-based, bird watching programs at elementary and middle schools throughout the Monterey Bay area. The program is conducted in collaboration with UC Santa Cruz undergraduate interns and the Ken Norris Center for Natural History.
